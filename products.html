Granite

Granite is a common type of felsic intrusive igneous rock that is granular and phaneritic in texture.

Granites can be predominantly white, pink, or gray in color, depending on their mineralogy.

The word "granite" comes from the Latin granum, a grain, in reference to the coarse-grained structure of such a completely crystalline rock, although commonly the term "granite" is used to refer to a wider range of coarse-grained igneous rocks containing quartz and feldspar.
